# Archie — Terminology Audit

Synthesis of three persona evaluations (first-time Viewer reader, first-time Studio author/curator,
screen-reader + plain-language reviewer) against the shipped UI copy and the `CONTEXT.md` glossary.
One row per term, ordered by clarity impact (the strings that most block understanding come first).

**Reading the verdicts:**
- **keep** — the word is the right one; any fix is to surrounding framing, not the noun itself.
- **reconsider** — the word is defensible but standalone labels need a teaching gloss or a context-dependent decision; flagged where a terminology call is pending (base/Reading/Object).
- **replace** — raw internal/schema/protocol vocabulary that genuinely blocks the relevant persona, with a clearly better plain word available.

Copy is sentence-case and hardcoded in components (no i18n catalog). Code identifiers
(`record.layers`, `noteLogicalId`, etc.) are out of scope — only user-visible runtime strings are judged here.

## Notes for the team

- **The schema/protocol cluster (Manifest, AnnotationList, AnnotationPage, TextualBody, fragment, HTTP) is the highest-value work** and is mostly confined to two import paths (`wadm-import.ts`, `iiif-import`) plus two fetch errors. Fixing those skip-reasons and errors removes nearly all the genuine jargon leakage with low blast radius.
- **HTTP and Studio working store are the two leaks that can reach a pure reader** (Viewer `published.ts` and `Gallery.svelte`) — prioritise those even though they're small.
- **Three pending terminology decisions** (Object, base/Reading wording, Playground/Project a.k.a. the "Local" badge) are flagged `reconsider` rather than `replace`: each is a load-bearing model noun where the personas split, so the call belongs to the team, not a unilateral rename.
- **The library / project / archive collision** is a one-concept-three-names problem the screen-reader persona caught most sharply; resolving it is naming hygiene, not a rename of "library."
- Punctuation tells (em-dashes in short strings like "Skip — stay Anonymous", "— None (base) —") are a separate copy-polish pass, not terminology.
