Group your notes two ways

Drawing a Note is the obvious part. The non-obvious part is how you organise them — and Archie gives two opposite tools.

Readingsexclusive A curated lens. A Note belongs to one Reading or none. The reader switches lenses in a legend — e.g. Cipher vs Hoax vs Natural-language. For rival interpretations that shouldn't mix.
Tagsadditive Plain labels — stack as many per Note as you like. The reader filters by them — e.g. watermark, paleography. For cross-cutting discovery.

Connect and weight them

Two more moves you'd never find by clicking around:

Cite — ⌘K While writing a note or section, press ⌘K (or pick visually) to drop a link to another Note or Exhibit. The link lands the reader right in that spot.
Emphasis Mark a note Normal or Strong to control how loud its marker reads on the canvas — guide the eye without clutter.
Why it matters for citation Every Note keeps a stable identity across edits, so a link — or a citation someone else makes to your note — stays valid after you revise it. On publish, each note gets its own durable anchor URL.
Try it Open a note, set a Reading and add a Tag, then publish-preview: toggle the Readings legend and filter by your tag to see both behaviours.
