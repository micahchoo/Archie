# Narrative Section model: a self-contained "third layer", not a tour of Notes

**Context & decision.** A **Section** is one ordered unit of a Narrative exhibit's reading spine (an IIIF `Range`). Building the Studio section-authoring + the publish↔read round-trip surfaced that "what a Section *is*" had never been decided — three parts of the system silently assumed different models (the `NarrativeEditor` treated a Section as self-contained; the bidar fixture derived Sections 1:1 from Notes; the Viewer's `NarrativeReader` coupled section *i* to note *i* by list position). Two coherent models were possible: **(A)** a Section is a *self-contained reading beat* — `{ objectId, start, prose }`, a camera target + prose authored over the objects, **independent of the annotation (Note) layer**; or **(B)** a Section is a *pointer into the Notes* — a curated walk whose focus is inherited from a referenced Note. **Decided: (A).** A Section carries its own `objectId`, its own `start` (the camera target), and its own `prose`. `start` is a **media fragment** interpreted by the object's media type — `xywh=…` for an image object, `t=start,end` for an AV object (the same grammar a Note's selector uses), replacing the spatial-only `xywh` field. The narrative spine may **switch objects** across sections; single-object is the degenerate case where every Section shares one `objectId`.

**The argument.** (A) is what CONTEXT already implied — "`start` = what the canvas shows," a camera, not a Note reference. Crucially **(A) subsumes (B)**: a note-walk is expressible as Sections whose `start` is a Note's region and whose prose ⌘K-cites that Note (the intra-Library linkability already shipped) — so the desire "this section is about Note X" is met by a *link in the prose*, never a structural field. (A) keeps the **narrative layer and the annotation layer independent**: a curator can compose a prose essay that tours chosen regions while the Notes remain a separately-explorable marker layer, and a multi-object or AV narrative falls out for free (the camera switches objects / focuses a time range). While a Section is active the Viewer shows **that object's** markers; §122 progressive-reveal is deferred to v1.1 and re-specified under (A) as "markers whose region the narrative has passed" (the original "markers appear *with their section*" definition presumed the rejected coupling and no longer has a referent). An authoring convenience — **"add section from a Note"** (seed a Section's `start` + prose from an existing Note) — gives (B)'s ease *inside* (A)'s model, neutralizing (A)'s only real cost.

## Rejected alternatives
- **(B) Section = a reference to a Note ("tour of notes").** Tempting because bidar — our one real narrative — *is* a 1:1 walk of its Notes. Rejected: it **welds the narrative layer to the annotation layer** (you cannot author a beat that isn't already a Note, e.g. a "step back and see the whole map" interstitial), and it cannot focus a region or time that no Note occupies. bidar is reframed, not privileged: its Sections carry explicit `start` regions lifted from each Note's geometry; the map's markers and the prose spine are the same content offered as two affordances (pins to explore, spine to read).
- **Section *i* ↔ Note *i* by list position** (the Viewer's accidental implementation). Rejected as incoherent: it ignores the Section's own `objectId`/`start`, so it renders only the first object, never switches canvases, and navigates by note-order rather than the authored target. It happened to work for bidar solely because bidar's Sections were derived from its Notes in order.
- **Spatial-only `start` (defer temporal).** Rejected: having just made object-switching first-class, leaving `start` spatial-biased would make a mixed image+AV narrative unable to point at a moment on the recording; the media-fragment grammar costs nothing extra (Notes already use it).

**Consequence (implementation).** Core `Section.xywh` → `Section.start` (full fragment); `toRanges` emits `canvasId#${start}`; `sectionsFromManifest` parses any fragment. The Viewer `NarrativeReader` must drive the canvas from the *active Section's* resolved target (switch object on `objectId` change, fitBounds the `start`), deleting the index coupling. The Studio `NarrativeEditor` gains a temporal `start` field for AV objects + the "add from Note" shortcut. Cited as `archie-narrative` Q-1; CONTEXT §Section sharpened inline.
