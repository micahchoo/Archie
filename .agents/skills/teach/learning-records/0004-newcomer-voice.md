# Onboarding voice: write for someone who doesn't know the concepts yet

User feedback 2026-06-20 on Step 1's section-2 copy ("There is no 'narrative mode' switch…
Add a Section…"): "the tone of this is like the user knows or cares about what narrative
sections are." It led with an insider "surprising rule" framing and used terms (Section,
narrative) before the newcomer had any reason to care.

Fix: reframed from the *visitor's/author's experience* — "Two ways an exhibit can greet a
visitor: a grid to browse, or a walk-through with your words alongside" — then named the term
(Section/narrative) gently and deferred it ("you'll write your first one later"). Captions
dropped component jargon (prose spine / deep-zoom canvas / Readings legend) in favour of what
the visitor gets.

Standing rule (now in [[NOTES.md]]): in onboarding, never assume the reader knows or cares about
a concept yet. Lead with experience and motivation; introduce Archie terms only when needed and
flag them as "later"; reserve component/affordance names for the step that actually teaches them.
Avoid "the rule that surprises everyone" / "there is no X switch" — a beginner never held that
assumption to be corrected.
