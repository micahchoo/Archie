Do's & Don'ts 07

Hold The Line

Do

  • Lay the verdant ground plus grain on every surface — the atmosphere is the system; flat color reads as a wireframe.
  • Keep moss as the default display and body figure; reserve emerald for the catalogue numeral on parchment and one selective letter-tint.
  • Set marquee and lineup in FOP VHS, upright, uppercase; let the glitch character carry the lean.
  • Lockup the catalogue stamp on a hunter band so the mint numeral reads, or fall back to emerald on bare parchment.
  • Signal elevation with moss translucency tiers, bloom, and grain — never a shadow; show the 2px emerald focus ring.
  • Respect prefers-reduced-motion: disable the bloom drift and the button hover transition.

Don't

  • Don't add a box-shadow anywhere — not even for focus. Depth comes from atmosphere and the offset emerald ring.
  • Don't set mint as type on bare parchment — at ≈1.3:1 it vanishes; mint is for dark-green surfaces only.
  • Don't use emerald for small body text — it fails contrast at body scale. Keep it to headline scale and fills.
  • Don't faux-skew FOP VHS or set the display in sentence case; the marquee is always upright uppercase.
  • Don't fill shapes with solid color beyond the emerald ticket button — no solid-fill pills or badges.
  • Don't let type sit on the busy mesh without a scrim — the canopy lines will fight the letters.
